The Digital Competition Bill, 2026, introduces an "ex-ante" regulatory framework to prevent anti-competitive conduct before it occurs in digital markets.

The financial strength test for SSDE status also includes a global market capitalization threshold of $75 billion or a gross merchandise value in India of ₹16,000 crore.
To meet the "significant spread" test, a core digital service must have at least 1 crore end users or 10,000 business users in India.
Group entities of an SSDE involved in providing core digital services are designated as Associate Digital Enterprises (ADEs) and share the same compliance obligations.
SSDEs are strictly prohibited from "self-preferencing," which involves favoring their own products or services over those of third-party business users.
The Bill bans "anti-steering" practices, ensuring SSDEs do not restrict business users from communicating offers directly to their end users.

Cross-use or mixing of personal data collected from different services within the same group is prohibited without explicit user consent.
The Competition Commission of India (CCI) is empowered to designate enterprises as SSDEs based on qualitative criteria even if they miss quantitative thresholds.
Non-compliance with the core obligations under the Digital Competition Bill can result in civil penalties up to 10% of the enterprise's global turnover.
